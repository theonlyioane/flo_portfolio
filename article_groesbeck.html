GROESBECK LODGE

RESIDENTIAL

"Center for Conservation and Stewardship"

CONCEPT

Setting the scenery inward and preserving the natural built elements. While creating the persona of an open clean lined space around large corridors. We will be led to living within nature through the juxtaposed Arts and Crafts finishes around.
Basking in the refection of the warm amber, and soothing white elements to offset each other. Finding a place to reflect within the landscape. While the cool crisp air entices your taste buds. Your feelings will take you back to a time where technology did not matter most, by your surroundings and feeding its thirst to live freely. Detoxing the world of many heinous elements.

PROBLEM

The Cincinnati Nature Center (CNC) acquired a two-and-a-half-story Tudor Revival fieldstone country house, from the Groesbeck’s. The CNC would like to use this place as an educational, event/ meeting space, and retail area for their visitors. But, keep some of the historic characteristics of the property.

CONCLUSION

The main floor of the lodge is used for retail and entertainment and the upper floor is for management and working personnel’s. The bathrooms are white to provide elegance and for sanitary reasons. The paint in the interiors reflect the outside scenery around the lodge. The floors are mainly made up of bamboo wood in an amber color. The bridal suite has a wall of large windows so the bridal party may enjoy the view of the property. This property is designed so the visitors may enjoy the outside scenery with the interior scenery.
